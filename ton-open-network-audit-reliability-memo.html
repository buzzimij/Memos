IWhat Is The Open Network (TON)?

The Open Network (TON) is a Layer 1 blockchain designed for massive scale — its architecture is explicitly built to support hundreds of thousands of transactions per second (TPS) through an infinite sharding paradigm, where the network can dynamically split into parallel processing units as demand grows.1 TON uses Proof of Stake (PoS) consensus and its native token is Toncoin (TON).

What makes TON strategically distinctive from virtually every other blockchain is its exclusive integration with Telegram — the messaging platform with over 900 million active users worldwide. Since January 2025, TON became the exclusive blockchain for all Telegram Mini Apps (TMAs), giving it distribution at a scale that most blockchain ecosystems take years to reach organically.2

✓
CPA Frame of Reference

Imagine Visa's payment network — but instead of being built by a bank consortium, it was built by the company that owns the messaging platform that half the world uses for daily communication. That is TON's strategic position: infrastructure designed from the ground up for massive throughput, now exclusively wired into Telegram's billion-user distribution channel. The audit implication is that Toncoin has a uniquely observable demand driver — Telegram Mini App transaction volume — that most cryptocurrencies lack.

IIHistory: From Telegram to Community Chain
OriginTelegram's Blockchain Project (2018–2020)

TON was originally conceived and built by Nikolai Durov (Telegram co-founder and technical lead) and his team as "Telegram Open Network" beginning in 2018.3 Telegram raised approximately $1.7 billion in an initial coin offering (ICO) in 2018 from accredited investors — at the time one of the largest ICOs in history. The token was called "GRAM."

In October 2019, the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) filed an emergency action against Telegram, alleging that the GRAM ICO was an unregistered securities offering. In June 2020, Telegram reached a settlement with the SEC, paid an $18.5 million penalty, returned approximately $1.2 billion to investors, and abandoned the project entirely.4

RebirthOpen-Source Community Continuation (2020–Present)

After Telegram's withdrawal, an open-source community called the "TON Foundation" adopted the codebase and relaunched the network independently. The GRAM token was renamed Toncoin (TON), and the blockchain relaunched without Telegram's direct involvement or ownership. Critically, Telegram later re-engaged with the TON ecosystem on a commercial basis — endorsing TON as Telegram's official blockchain for payment and Mini App infrastructure — without having formal ownership or control of the TON protocol itself.5

ℹ️
Important Governance Distinction

Telegram does not own or control the TON blockchain. The TON Foundation (an independent open-source organization) governs the protocol. Telegram's relationship with TON is a commercial partnership — Telegram chose TON as its exclusive blockchain for Mini Apps, but it cannot alter TON's protocol parameters, validator set, or token supply unilaterally. This distinction is important for risk assessments: Telegram-specific regulatory actions would affect TON's distribution channel but not its underlying protocol integrity.

IIIArchitecture: Masterchain, Workchains & Shardchains

TON's architecture is among the most technically ambitious in blockchain design. Understanding it at a high level is important for auditors because it determines both the capabilities and the evidence-gathering complexity of the network.6

StructureThree-Level Hierarchy
Chain LevelFunctionQuantityAccounting Analogy
Masterchain Global state coordination; records validator set, protocol parameters, and cryptographic hashes of all workchain states One (1) The general ledger — the authoritative record that other ledgers (workchains) report into
Workchains Independent blockchains that operate in parallel; each can have custom rules (e.g., different VM, smart contract language, asset types) Up to 232 theoretically; currently one primary workchain (Workchain 0) Subsidiary ledgers — separate books of record that feed into the masterchain general ledger
Shardchains Sub-divisions of workchains that process transactions for a subset of accounts in parallel; shards split and merge dynamically based on load Up to 260 per workchain theoretically Departmental sub-ledgers — each handles a subset of transactions, periodically reconciling to the workchain
Key ConceptDynamic Sharding vs. Fixed Sharding

Most blockchain sharding implementations (including Ethereum's planned approach) use a fixed number of shards. TON uses dynamic sharding: shardchains automatically split when a shard becomes overloaded and merge when traffic drops. This means TON theoretically has no hard upper bound on processing capacity — capacity scales with network demand.7

For auditors: dynamic sharding means that the "database" being audited does not have a fixed structure. A complete audit trail of all transactions requires querying potentially multiple shardchains that may have existed at different points in time. This adds complexity relative to single-chain networks like Ethereum.

IVConsensus, Performance & Toncoin
ConsensusByzantine Fault Tolerant Proof of Stake

TON uses a variant of Proof of Stake consensus with Byzantine Fault Tolerance (BFT). Validators are selected based on their Toncoin stake and must reach a supermajority agreement (2/3+) to finalize blocks. Block finality is near-instant — approximately 5 seconds — which is significantly faster than Ethereum's ~12-second slot time.8

The validator set is currently approximately 300 active validators, a relatively small set for a major public blockchain. Validator participation requires a minimum stake of 300,000 Toncoin and a stake deposit in the ElectorSC (Elector Smart Contract), TON's built-in validator management system.

PerformanceThroughput Claims

TON has demonstrated over 100,000 TPS in controlled test conditions.9 For context: Visa's network processes approximately 24,000 TPS at peak; Ethereum processes approximately 15–30 TPS. The 100,000 TPS figure is achieved through parallel shardchain processing and should be understood as a theoretical maximum under optimized conditions, not necessarily real-world sustained throughput during stress events.

ToncoinToken Characteristics for Financial Reporting

Toncoin (TON) is the native token of The Open Network, used for gas fees, validator staking, and as a payment medium within the Telegram ecosystem.10 Key characteristics relevant to FASB ASC 350-60:

  • Freely transferable ERC-20 equivalent (using TON's FunC language contracts); listed on major centralized exchanges (Binance, OKX, Bybit, KuCoin)
  • Observable market price — clear Level 1 fair value input per ASC 820
  • Maximum supply: 5 billion TON; approximately 3.4 billion in circulation as of April 2026
  • Inflationary: validator staking rewards are minted as new Toncoin annually; deflationary pressure comes from gas fee burning
VTON in Practice: Telegram Integration & Cocoon
MilestoneTelegram Mini Apps — January 2025

In January 2025, Telegram announced that TON would become the exclusive blockchain for all Telegram Mini Apps (TMAs).11 Mini Apps are lightweight web applications that run natively inside the Telegram client — games, exchanges, payment processors, social platforms — and can process Toncoin payments directly within the chat interface. This created an unprecedented blockchain distribution scenario: ~900 million Telegram users became potential TON users through their existing app.

The financial reporting implication is significant: many businesses now accept Toncoin as payment through Telegram Mini Apps. These transactions are on-chain and generate Toncoin inflows that require recognition and measurement under ASC 350-60. The volume of such transactions may be auditable by querying the TON blockchain directly.

EcosystemCocoon: Decentralized AI Compute — December 2025

In December 2025, the Cocoon project launched on TON mainnet.12 Cocoon (Confidential Compute Open Network) is a decentralized GPU computing marketplace built on TON — GPU owners contribute compute capacity, and buyers (AI workloads, model training) pay in Toncoin. Computing is performed inside Trusted Execution Environments (TEEs) — hardware-level secure enclaves that prevent the compute operator from accessing the data being processed, preserving confidentiality.

ℹ️
Accounting Note — Cocoon Revenue Recognition

For entities that earn Toncoin by providing GPU capacity to Cocoon, each compute job completed represents a revenue event — Toncoin received in exchange for a service. The entity must determine: (1) whether the service has been performed (compute job completed per TEE attestation); (2) the Toncoin fair value at the time of service completion per ASC 350-60; (3) whether the Toncoin is immediately sold or held as a crypto asset. Each of these steps requires audit evidence from both on-chain data and operational records.

⚠️
Shardchain Evidence Complexity

Unlike Ethereum where all transactions are on a single chain, TON transactions may be processed across multiple shardchains that existed at different points in time. A complete transaction history may require querying multiple shardchain states. Standard block explorers may not provide complete coverage — auditors handling material TON balances should confirm evidence completeness by cross-referencing the masterchain's cryptographic commitments to workchain/shardchain state roots.

VIIInstitutional Profile & ASC 350-60 Considerations
ProfileNetwork Scale & Market Standing

As of April 2026, Toncoin consistently ranks in the top 10 cryptocurrencies by market capitalization, reflecting its unique distribution advantage through Telegram.13 The TON Foundation maintains significant treasury holdings of Toncoin, and validator staking is sufficiently distributed to avoid single-entity dominance. The network has operated without a major security incident since its community relaunch in 2020.

The SEC's 2020 action was against Telegram's GRAM token offering, not against the TON blockchain protocol or the community-relaunched Toncoin. Toncoin is a distinct token issued by a different entity under different circumstances. However, auditors should note the historical regulatory context when assessing residual regulatory risk.

AccountingFASB ASC 350-60 Application

Toncoin's accounting treatment under FASB ASC 350-60 (effective fiscal years beginning after December 15, 2024) is relatively straightforward compared to some other digital assets:14

  • Classification: Toncoin is an in-scope crypto asset — fungible, not a security under current U.S. law, and does not provide the holder with rights to goods or services from the issuer
  • Measurement: Fair value through net income; Level 1 observable price from major exchanges (Binance, OKX)
  • Disclosure: Entities must disclose Toncoin holdings, cost basis, fair value gains/losses recognized in income, and the exchange price source used
  • Staking rewards: New Toncoin earned through validator staking is recognized as income at fair value on the date received; subsequently measured under ASC 350-60

IPurpose of This Memorandum

This memorandum evaluates the reliability and audit risk profile of The Open Network (TON) blockchain and Toncoin for audit and assurance purposes. It assists engagement teams in assessing whether Toncoin balances, transaction histories, and staking income are supported by sufficient appropriate audit evidence, and evaluates key structural risks including TON's multi-chain architecture, Telegram concentration risk, and regulatory history.

✓
Bottom Line Up Front

TON's underlying blockchain infrastructure is technically mature and its Toncoin has clear Level 1 observable market prices — making it one of the more straightforward digital assets for ASC 350-60 measurement. The primary audit risks are: (1) multi-chain evidence complexity requiring indexing APIs rather than simple block explorer queries; (2) concentration risk from Telegram's exclusive distribution relationship; and (3) residual regulatory history from the 2020 SEC action (against Telegram's GRAM, not community Toncoin).

IIBackground & Scope
BackgroundProtocol Overview

The Open Network (TON) is a Layer 1 PoS blockchain originally designed by Telegram co-founder Nikolai Durov, abandoned by Telegram following a 2020 SEC settlement, and continued independently by the TON Foundation open-source community. Toncoin is the native token. In January 2025, Telegram designated TON as the exclusive blockchain for its Mini App ecosystem, creating a direct link between TON transactions and Telegram's ~900M user base. In December 2025, Cocoon launched on TON mainnet as a decentralized AI compute network backed by Toncoin payments.

ScopeScope of This Analysis

This assessment covers: (a) on-chain data integrity and multi-chain evidence considerations; (b) consensus mechanism reliability; (c) Telegram dependency and ecosystem concentration risks; (d) Toncoin classification under FASB ASC 350-60; (e) identified documentation and evidence gaps. Market risk, price volatility, and non-U.S. regulatory compliance are excluded from scope.

IIIData Integrity & Multi-Chain Evidence
Overall Assessment: ● Moderate — Complexity Elevated by Architecture

TON's on-chain data is immutable and verifiable — the masterchain provides cryptographic commitments to all workchain and shardchain states, creating a provably complete root of trust. However, TON's infinite sharding architecture means that a complete transaction history may be distributed across multiple shardchains, requiring more sophisticated evidence-gathering than single-chain networks.

PositiveReliability Indicators
  • Masterchain provides cryptographic (hash-based) commitments to all workchain states — the master record is tamper-evident
  • BFT consensus provides near-instant block finality (~5 seconds); no probabilistic settlement risk
  • Multiple independent block explorers operate (tonscan.org, tonviewer.com) providing redundant verification sources
  • TON Center API provides programmatic access to full transaction history across shardchains
  • Network has operated since 2020 community launch without a protocol-level security failure
Risk FactorsEvidence Gathering Complexity
  • Shardchain evidence fragmentation: Transactions involving smart contracts may be split across multiple shardchains; standard block explorer confirmation of a wallet balance does not necessarily capture all associated smart contract interactions
  • FunC smart contract language: TON uses a unique programming language (FunC) and a cell-based data structure (Bag of Cells / BoC) distinct from Ethereum's Solidity; most audit firms' smart contract review teams may lack TON-specific expertise
  • Message routing complexity: TON uses asynchronous message passing between contracts rather than synchronous calls; tracing multi-step transactions requires understanding this message queue architecture
IVConsensus Mechanism & Governance
Overall Assessment: ● Moderate-High

TON's BFT PoS consensus is a well-tested paradigm, and the ElectorSC (validator management contract) provides a transparent, on-chain mechanism for validator selection and reward distribution. The primary governance concern is the relatively small active validator set (~300 validators) compared to Ethereum (~1,000,000 validators).

ValidatorsValidator Set Assessment
  • ~300 active validators; validator selection based on Toncoin stake deposited in ElectorSC
  • Minimum stake: 300,000 TON (approximately $1.5M–$3M depending on Toncoin price) — high entry barrier reduces validator count but also reduces sybil attack risk
  • BFT threshold: 2/3+ of validators must agree to finalize a block; a coordinated attack requires compromising >100 validators holding >67% of staked Toncoin
  • Validator incentives: block rewards paid in new Toncoin minted per block; aligned with long-term network health
GovernanceProtocol Governance Structure

The TON Foundation governs protocol changes through a combination of off-chain community voting (governance forum) and validator signaling. There is no fully on-chain governance mechanism comparable to MakerDAO or Compound — protocol upgrades are ultimately implemented by validators choosing to run updated software. This is similar to Bitcoin's governance model: rough consensus + validator adoption, not a formal on-chain vote.

⚠️
TON Foundation Concentration

The TON Foundation and early supporters hold a significant proportion of total Toncoin supply. While the exact current foundation treasury balance requires current verification, historically significant allocations to the foundation create a governance concentration risk: the foundation's decisions on treasury Toncoin usage (including sales) can materially impact Toncoin market dynamics.

VTelegram Dependency & Ecosystem Risk
Overall Assessment: ● Elevated — Concentration Risk

TON's most distinctive strategic asset is simultaneously its most significant concentration risk: the exclusive commercial relationship with Telegram. Virtually all of TON's retail user growth and transaction volume growth since 2025 is attributable to the Telegram Mini App integration. This creates a binary dependency:

VIToken Classification & Financial Reporting
Overall Assessment: ● Moderate-High — Relatively Straightforward

Toncoin's accounting treatment under FASB ASC 350-60 is among the more straightforward in our digital asset protocol review series. Key conclusions:

ClassificationASC 350-60 Analysis
  • In scope: Toncoin is fungible, transferable, listed on major exchanges, and does not convey rights to goods or services from the issuer — the standard ASC 350-60 criteria are satisfied
  • Fair value measurement: Level 1 input under ASC 820 — Toncoin trades on Binance, OKX, Bybit, and KuCoin with significant daily volume; multiple active market price sources are available
  • Staking income: Validator staking rewards are recognized as income at fair value on date received; subsequently measured at fair value through net income under ASC 350-60
  • Cocoon compute income: Toncoin received for GPU compute services is revenue (not a crypto asset gain/loss) at the time the service is performed; recognize at fair value on service completion date per ASC 606 principles
HistoryRegulatory Classification Context

The 2020 SEC action concerned Telegram's GRAM token — which was deemed an unregistered security at issuance due to the profit-expectation framework of the ICO. The community-issued Toncoin was not issued by Telegram and was not the subject of that action. However, no formal SEC staff no-action letter or guidance specifically addressing Toncoin's non-security status has been published. Entities holding significant Toncoin should consult legal counsel regarding current U.S. regulatory classification.

VIIGaps & Limitations

The following gaps represent areas where available information is insufficient for definitive audit conclusions without additional procedures:

  • ⚠
    Multi-Chain Evidence Completeness. Standard block explorer confirmation of Toncoin balances may not capture all associated smart contract state changes across shardchains. For clients with material Mini App or smart contract exposure, completeness of on-chain evidence requires verification via TON indexer API.
  • ⚠
    No Published Formal Security Audit of Core Protocol. Unlike some Layer 1 networks, no Big 4 or top-tier smart contract firm has published a comprehensive protocol-level security audit covering TON's core consensus and cross-chain messaging layer that we have identified.
  • ⚠
    Telegram Partnership Terms Undisclosed. The specific commercial terms of Telegram's exclusive TON integration are not publicly disclosed. Clients whose Toncoin valuation thesis is based on Telegram volume should recognize this undisclosed dependency as a material uncertainty.
  • ⚠
    No SEC No-Action Letter for Toncoin. While the community-issued Toncoin is legally distinct from Telegram's GRAM, no formal U.S. regulatory guidance or no-action letter has been published confirming its non-security status.
  • ⚠
    FunC/Smart Contract Audit Expertise. Few audit firms have staff with TON FunC smart contract expertise. Clients with material exposure to TON smart contracts (Mini Apps, Cocoon) should obtain independent FunC smart contract review before placing reliance on smart contract-generated balances.
VIIIRecommended Audit Procedures

The following procedures respond to the identified risks, consistent with PCAOB AS 1105 (as amended December 2025) and AICPA Digital Assets Practice Guide guidance.

Balance & Transaction Verification
  • 1
    Multi-Level On-Chain Balance Confirmation

    Confirm Toncoin wallet balances using at least two independent block explorers (tonscan.org and tonviewer.com). For clients with smart contract interactions, additionally query the TON Center API or an equivalent indexer for complete shardchain transaction history. Document the query method, shardchain coverage scope, and confirmation as of a specific masterchain block height.

  • 2
    Staking Reward Income Verification

    Obtain the client's validator staking address and query ElectorSC reward events. Verify that staking income is recorded at Toncoin fair value on the date each reward was received. Compare total staking rewards per on-chain ElectorSC events to the amount recorded in the income statement.

Risk Assessment & Classification
  • 3
    Telegram Dependency Disclosure Assessment

    For clients whose Toncoin holdings or business operations are materially dependent on Telegram's Mini App platform, assess whether going concern or concentration risk disclosures are required. Review publicly available information on Telegram's regulatory status in key jurisdictions of operation.

  • 4
    Fair Value Price Source Documentation

    Document the Toncoin price source selected for ASC 350-60 fair value measurement. Confirm that the selected exchange qualifies as an "active market" per ASC 820. Perform spot-price comparison across Binance, OKX, and at least one aggregator (CoinMarketCap or CoinGecko) to confirm price reasonableness within 1% variance.

  • 5
    Mini App / Cocoon Revenue Recognition Testing

    For clients receiving Toncoin through Telegram Mini Apps or Cocoon compute contracts, test revenue recognition: confirm service delivery evidence (Mini App order records; Cocoon TEE attestation), confirm Toncoin receipt on-chain, confirm fair value recorded at service completion date, and verify Toncoin subsequently measured at period-end fair value per ASC 350-60.

Legal & Regulatory Review
  • 6
    Regulatory Classification Legal Opinion

    For clients with material Toncoin exposure, obtain or review external legal counsel opinion on whether community-issued Toncoin constitutes a security under current U.S. federal law. Note that no formal SEC staff guidance or no-action letter has been published specifically addressing community Toncoin. Document legal counsel's conclusion and basis.
