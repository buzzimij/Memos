IWhat Is Berachain?

Berachain is a Layer 1 blockchain launched on mainnet February 6, 2025.1 It is built on the Cosmos SDK framework — the same underlying toolkit that powers Cosmos Hub, Celestia, and dozens of other high-performance blockchains — but is EVM-compatible, meaning it runs the same Solidity smart contracts and Ethereum-compatible developer tooling as Ethereum itself.2

The network was created by an anonymous team known for the "Bong Bears" NFT collection and is distinguished primarily by its novel consensus mechanism: Proof of Liquidity (PoL), which replaces idle staking with active capital deployment into protocol-approved liquidity pools. The network's three-token architecture (BERA, BGT, HONEY) is deliberately designed to separate the functions of gas payment, governance, and stablecoin utility — an intentional economic design choice with significant financial reporting implications.3

✓
CPA Frame of Reference

Think of Berachain as a new financial market infrastructure (analogous to a clearinghouse or exchange). It runs on proven technology (Cosmos SDK ≈ an established financial software framework) but introduces a novel market mechanism requiring that participants deploy capital productively rather than hold it idle. Infrared Finance is the specialized intermediary (≈ a prime broker or custodian) that sits between retail participants and this system, making its benefits accessible without requiring direct technical participation.

IIProof of Liquidity Consensus Mechanism
ConceptHow PoL Differs from Traditional Proof of Stake

Traditional Proof of Stake (PoS) requires validators to lock up the native token in a smart contract (called "staking"). Those tokens sit idle earning block rewards but providing no economic utility to the network beyond security bonding. Berachain's Proof of Liquidity changes this requirement: validators must deposit liquidity into protocol-approved PoL Vaults — smart contracts that accept LP (Liquidity Provider) tokens from Automated Market Makers — to earn BGT (Block Governance Token) emissions and receive block-proposing rights.4

The effect is that validator "stake" is simultaneously functioning as liquidity for the DeFi ecosystem, creating a structural alignment between network security and ecosystem growth.

ℹ️
Accounting Analogy

Traditional PoS is like requiring clearing members to post cash margin that sits in a segregated escrow account — secured but idle. PoL is like requiring them to invest that margin in approved money-market instruments within a fund. The capital is still secured, but it is simultaneously deployed, generating yield for the ecosystem. Withdrawing liquidity is the "margin call" equivalent in PoL.

MechanicsBGT Emission Flow

The PoL incentive loop works as follows:5

  1. Users deposit LP tokens into PoL Vaults (approved by governance)
  2. Validators operate vaults and earn BGT proportional to their vault's total value locked (TVL)
  3. BGT is distributed as block rewards — non-transferable, only earnable through this mechanism
  4. Protocols seeking to attract BGT emissions pay validators (or protocols like Infrared) to direct emissions to their vaults — creating a secondary "BGT incentive marketplace"
  5. BGT holders can burn BGT 1:1 for BERA (the liquid gas token) or delegate BGT back to validators to influence future emission direction
IIIThree-Token Architecture

Berachain's deliberate separation of economic functions across three native tokens is unusual for a Layer 1 blockchain. Each token has distinct characteristics with direct financial reporting implications under FASB ASC 350-60.6

⚠️
BGT — Unique Accounting Challenge

BGT's non-transferability is a protocol-level constraint, not a contractual lock-up. There is no observable market price and no mechanism by which it can be sold. Current FASB ASC 350-60 guidance covers crypto assets with observable prices — BGT almost certainly falls outside its scope. Entities holding BGT must work with their auditors and technical accounting advisors to determine appropriate classification and disclosure. The AICPA's Digital Assets Practice Guide and any applicable SEC staff guidance should be consulted.

IVInfrared Finance: The Liquid Staking Layer
ProblemThe BGT Liquidity Problem

Because BGT is non-transferable, participants who earn it through PoL vault participation face an illiquidity problem: they cannot use BGT as collateral in DeFi lending protocols, sell it on exchanges, or transfer it to another wallet. Their only options are to delegate it (to influence BGT emissions) or burn it 1:1 for BERA. This severely limits the capital efficiency of BGT holdings for sophisticated participants who want to use those holdings in the broader DeFi ecosystem.

SolutionInfrared's Four-Token Ecosystem

Infrared Finance, the largest liquid staking protocol on Berachain, solves this problem by acting as an intermediary between users and the PoL vault system.7 Infrared manages PoL vault participation, earns BGT on users' behalf, and issues liquid derivative tokens in return. The protocol introduces four tokens on top of Berachain's three:

✓
Key Audit Insight — iBGT Reserve Adequacy

iBGT is only as sound as the BGT reserves held in Infrared's smart contracts. Auditors should independently verify the iBGT:BGT backing ratio by querying the totalSupply() of the iBGT contract against the BGT balance of Infrared's vault contract. Any ratio below 1:1 represents an unbacked issuance — the equivalent of a reserve shortfall in a stablecoin issuer.

Infrared Finance has published smart contract audit reports from Trail of Bits and Pashov Audit Group.8 Trail of Bits is widely considered one of the most rigorous smart contract security firms in the industry, adding credibility to reported findings. Auditors should obtain and review these reports, document any open findings, and request management's remediation evidence.

⚠️
BGT Incentive Marketplace — Payment-for-Order-Flow Analogy

The practice of protocols paying Infrared (or validators) to direct BGT emissions to their specific vaults is structurally analogous to payment-for-order-flow (PFOF) in traditional securities markets: a third party compensates the routing intermediary to direct flow their way. These arrangements may be economically material but are conducted off-chain via private agreements. They create an audit evidence gap that cannot be resolved solely by blockchain data.

VIIInstitutional Profile & ASC 350-60 Considerations
FundingCapital Raise & Investor Profile

Infrared Finance raised $18.75 million in a seed round (announced February 2025), structured as SAFTs (Simple Agreements for Future Tokens).10 Lead investors included YZi Labs (formerly Binance Labs — the venture arm of Binance, the world's largest crypto exchange), Framework Ventures (a major DeFi-focused VC), and NGC Ventures. SAFT instruments have specific accounting implications: the issuer typically records deferred revenue (obligation to deliver tokens) until tokens are delivered, at which point revenue is recognized or reclassified based on token nature.

StandardsFASB ASC 350-60 Application Summary

FASB ASC 350-60, effective for fiscal years beginning after December 15, 2024, requires entities to measure crypto assets at fair value through net income when the asset has an observable market price on an active exchange.11 The Berachain token ecosystem creates a four-tier accounting treatment:

  • BERA — Observable market price; covered by ASC 350-60; fair value through P&L each reporting period
  • BGT — Non-transferable, no market; almost certainly outside ASC 350-60; likely classified as an intangible asset with no determinable fair value or as a performance obligation
  • iBGT — DEX-traded ERC-20; market may qualify as "active" under ASC 820 criteria; professional judgment required on market activity threshold
  • HONEY — Stablecoin; likely equivalent to a fiat-denominated receivable or cash equivalent if collateral is deemed sound; not a "crypto asset" per ASC 350-60 if it effectively functions as a digital dollar

IPurpose of This Memorandum

This memorandum evaluates the reliability and audit risk profile of the Berachain Layer 1 blockchain and Infrared Finance liquid staking protocol. It is intended to assist engagement teams in assessing whether transactions, balances, and disclosures related to Berachain-native assets (BERA, BGT, iBGT, HONEY, IR, sIR) are supported by sufficient appropriate audit evidence under PCAOB AS 1105 and AICPA attestation standards.

✓
Bottom Line Up Front

Berachain's on-chain transaction data is independently verifiable and provides high-quality audit evidence for BERA and iBGT balances. The primary audit risks are: (1) the novel PoL consensus mechanism with limited operational history; (2) BGT's non-transferable nature creating unresolved accounting treatment questions; and (3) off-chain BGT incentive arrangements that cannot be confirmed via blockchain data alone.

IIBackground & Scope
BackgroundProtocol Overview

Berachain is an EVM-compatible Layer 1 blockchain using Proof of Liquidity consensus, with mainnet launch February 6, 2025. Infrared Finance is the leading liquid staking protocol built on Berachain, providing iBGT (liquid BGT), iBERA (liquid BERA staking), IR (governance), and sIR (staked IR) to users seeking to maximize capital efficiency from PoL vault participation. Infrared raised $18.75M from YZi Labs, Framework Ventures, and NGC Ventures in a 2025 SAFT-structured seed round.

ScopeScope of This Analysis

This assessment covers: (a) on-chain data integrity for BERA, BGT, iBGT, HONEY, and IR transactions; (b) smart contract reliability based on published audit reports; (c) Proof of Liquidity consensus mechanism risk assessment; (d) token classification under FASB ASC 350-60; (e) documentation gaps. This memo does not address market risk, price volatility, investment suitability, or non-U.S. regulatory compliance.

IIIData Integrity & Immutability Assessment
Overall Assessment: ● Moderate-High

Berachain uses Byzantine Fault Tolerant (BFT) consensus via Tendermint Core (part of the Cosmos SDK). Tendermint provides immediate finality — once a block is committed, it is final. There is no probabilistic settlement risk (no concept of "waiting for confirmations"), which is a significant advantage over Proof of Work chains from an audit evidence perspective.

PositiveReliability Indicators
  • Transaction data is publicly accessible via Berascan (berascan.com) — Berachain's native block explorer
  • Immediate Tendermint finality eliminates deep-reorg risk that affects Bitcoin and pre-Merge Ethereum
  • Open-source code base; smart contract bytecode verifiable against published source code
  • EVM compatibility allows use of standard Ethereum developer tooling for independent state verification
Risk FactorsData Integrity Limitations
  • Limited operational history: Mainnet launched February 2025; only ~14 months of network history as of April 2026
  • Validator concentration risk: If a small number of validators control >33% of BGT weight, they can theoretically halt the chain; this risk is elevated in early-stage networks where BGT distribution is still concentrating
  • PoL vault data complexity: Reconstructing complete vault deposit history requires blockchain indexing beyond standard block explorers; auditors may need to use third-party indexing APIs or run their own node
IVSmart Contract Reliability
Overall Assessment: ● Moderate

Infrared Finance has published audit reports from Trail of Bits and Pashov Audit Group. Trail of Bits is widely regarded as one of the highest-credibility smart contract security firms in the industry. This represents a meaningful positive indicator of smart contract reliability relative to many DeFi protocols that operate without formal security reviews.

Key RisksSmart Contract Vulnerabilities
  • iBGT peg mechanism: The 1:1 iBGT:BGT backing ratio is enforced by smart contract logic, not an independent custodian. A bug in the minting or redemption contract could allow iBGT to be issued without corresponding BGT backing — equivalent to fractional-reserve issuance without disclosure
  • Cross-layer interaction risk: The interaction between Berachain's core PoL vault contracts and Infrared's wrapper contracts has not been separately published as a distinct audit scope item
  • IR/sIR governance risk: IR token distribution and sIR fee allocation are governed by multisig-controlled contracts; key-person risk if multisig signers are unknown or pseudonymous
  • Protocol upgrade risk: Berachain is a young network; protocol upgrades (governance-approved changes to core contracts) could alter economic parameters, affecting existing users
⛔
Documentation Gap

No SOC 2 Type II report, Big 4 attestation, or equivalent operational controls documentation has been published by Infrared Finance. For entities with material Infrared Finance exposure, auditors should document this gap and consider whether additional procedures (direct smart contract queries, management representations, or review of audit reports) provide sufficient evidence in the absence of formal attestation.

VConsensus Mechanism Risk
Overall Assessment: ● Moderate — Novel Mechanism

Proof of Liquidity is an experimental consensus overlay on the well-tested Tendermint BFT framework. The BFT layer is proven; the PoL incentive layer is novel and untested at scale over multiple market cycles.

RisksPoL-Specific Consensus Risks
  • BGT emission centralization: Sophisticated validators (or protocols like Infrared) that accumulate disproportionate BGT emissions gain outsized influence over future emission direction, potentially creating a self-reinforcing centralization dynamic
  • Incentive marketplace integrity: The off-chain market for BGT emission direction (protocols paying for emission targeting) operates outside the on-chain governance framework and creates economic relationships that auditors cannot verify solely through blockchain data
  • Early-stage governance concentration: The PoL Vault whitelist (which determines which protocols receive emissions) is initially governed by a foundation-controlled multisig, concentrating protocol-level decision-making
  • Market stress behavior: PoL vaults contain LP tokens whose value fluctuates with market conditions; a severe market downturn could reduce validator collateral value, potentially incentivizing withdrawal and destabilizing the consensus mechanism

VIIGaps & Limitations

The following gaps represent areas where available public information or published standards do not provide sufficient basis for definitive audit conclusions without additional procedures:

  • ⚠
    Limited Operating History. Mainnet launched February 2025 — only ~14 months of network data as of April 2026. Insufficient history to assess behavior under market stress or protocol upgrade scenarios. Heightened skepticism is appropriate.
  • ⚠
    Absence of SOC Report. No SOC 2 Type II or equivalent operational controls attestation has been published by Infrared Finance. This is a standard gap for DeFi protocols but remains a documentation deficiency for audit files.
  • ⚠
    BGT Accounting Treatment Unresolved. No authoritative FASB or SEC guidance addresses the classification of non-transferable, non-marketable governance tokens. Entities holding material BGT balances should obtain technical accounting memos and consider consulting FASB's technical inquiry process.
  • ⚠
    Off-chain BGT Incentive Deals. Protocol-to-protocol economic arrangements for BGT emission targeting are not disclosed on-chain. Their existence, value, and counterparties can only be confirmed through management inquiry and contractual review — not blockchain verification.
  • ⚠
    Validator Identity Opacity. Tendermint validators may operate under pseudonymous identities; counterparty identity risk is elevated relative to regulated financial infrastructure operators.
VIIIRecommended Audit Procedures

The following procedures respond to the identified risks and gaps, consistent with PCAOB AS 1105 (as amended December 2025) and AICPA Digital Assets Practice Guide guidance.

Balance Verification
  • 1
    Independent On-Chain Balance Confirmation

    Query Berascan (berascan.com) or directly query the Berachain node RPC endpoint to confirm client's BERA, iBGT, and HONEY balances as of the balance sheet date. Document the block number queried, timestamp, and data source per PCAOB AS 1105.10A.

  • 2
    iBGT Reserve Adequacy Verification

    Call totalSupply() on the iBGT contract and compare to BGT balance held by Infrared's vault contract. Any ratio below 1:1 (iBGT issued > BGT held) constitutes an unbacked issuance event requiring immediate escalation to engagement leadership.

Smart Contract & Protocol Risk
  • 3
    Audit Report Review & Open Finding Assessment

    Obtain Trail of Bits and Pashov Audit Group reports for Infrared's contracts. Document each finding's severity, status (open/remediated), and whether any open critical or high findings affect balances held by the client. Request management's remediation evidence for all findings.

  • 4
    Off-Chain Incentive Agreement Disclosure

    Request management to provide a complete schedule of all off-chain BGT emission incentive agreements entered into by the client or on the client's behalf. Assess whether any constitute related-party transactions requiring disclosure.

Accounting Classification
  • 5
    BGT Classification Technical Memo

    For entities with material BGT balances, prepare or obtain a technical accounting memo documenting the basis for the selected accounting classification (intangible with no determinable FV vs. other). Document consultation with FASB technical staff or national accounting office as applicable.

  • 6
    BERA Fair Value Documentation

    Document the selected price source for BERA fair value measurement (primary exchange, aggregator). Assess whether the market qualifies as "active" per ASC 820. Perform a spot-price comparison across at least two independent sources to confirm price reasonableness.

  • 7
    Validator Concentration Assessment

    Review BGT weight distribution across validators via Berascan or governance dashboard. Document whether any single validator or affiliated group controls >33% of BGT weight (network halt risk threshold). Flag material concentration for risk assessment purposes.

IXKey Takeaways
  • →
    Novel Mechanism, Young Network. PoL is theoretically sound but has only ~14 months of live operational history. Auditors should apply heightened professional skepticism and limit reliance on operational history as a risk indicator until the network has weathered at least one full market cycle.
  • →
    Six-Token Complexity Requires Per-Token Analysis. ASC 350-60 applies cleanly to BERA; BGT requires a separate non-standard accounting treatment; iBGT, IR, and sIR each require individual market-activity assessments; HONEY should be assessed as a stablecoin/digital cash instrument.
  • →
    iBGT Reserve Adequacy Is the Key Control. The 1:1 iBGT:BGT backing ratio is the central financial integrity control of the Infrared protocol. Direct smart contract verification of this ratio should be a standard procedure for any engagement with material iBGT exposure.
  • →
    Off-chain Governance Gaps Require Supplemental Evidence. The BGT emission incentive marketplace, validator selection strategy, and IR vesting arrangements are not fully reflected in on-chain data and require management inquiry, contractual review, and management representation letters to support audit conclusions.

Suggested Next Steps:

  • 1
    Perform iBGT reserve adequacy verification (Procedure 2) as a priority procedure for any client with material Infrared Finance exposure.
  • 2
    Engage technical accounting specialists (national office or external advisors) on BGT classification before issuing an opinion for entities with material BGT balances.
  • 3
    Maintain a rolling watch on Berachain governance decisions (PoL vault whitelist changes, core protocol upgrades) that could materially alter the risk profile between audit cycles.
